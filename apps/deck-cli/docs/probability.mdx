# Probability Analysis

## What this does

The `--stats` flag computes hypergeometric probabilities from your deck list — opening hand odds for every card, prize risk for singletons, and a T1-T4 draw curve. No game state is modelled. The inputs are copy count, deck size, and hand size; the output is exact math.

<Callout variant="info">
  Probability analysis runs entirely on the local MCP server. It does not call the Anthropic API and does not consume tokens.
</Callout>

## Using `--stats`

johto --deck ./decks/mega-sharpedo-ex-2.toml --stats

| Column | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `x` | Copy count in the deck |
| `open 1+` | P(at least 1 copy in opening hand of 7) |
| `T2` | P(at least 1 seen by end of Turn 2, going second) |
| `T3` | P(at least 1 seen by end of Turn 3) |
| `T4` | P(at least 1 seen by end of Turn 4) |

Cards marked with **★** are spotlight cards — high-impact singletons flagged automatically (ACE SPECs) or manually via `--spotlight`. The **⚠ Prize risk** section lists all singletons and their chance of being stuck in prizes.

<Callout variant="tip">
  `--stats` can be combined with `--dry-run` — only the probability table is printed, no system prompt, no REPL.
</Callout>

## Opening hand probability reference

For a complete 60-card deck with a 7-card opening hand:

| Copies | open 1+ | p(prized) |
|:------:|:-------:|:---------:|
| 1      | 11.7%   | 10.0%     |
| 2      | 22.1%   | 19.2%     |
| 3      | 31.5%   | 27.5%     |
| 4      | 39.9%   | 35.1%     |

<Callout variant="warning">
  These values assume a complete 60-card deck. The CLI computes probabilities on the actual deck size when `complete: false` — numbers will shift when missing slots are filled.
</Callout>

## Prize risk

Six cards are randomly removed from your deck before the game begins as prize cards. Any card stuck in prizes is inaccessible until you take a knockout.

- Singleton (1 copy): 10.0% chance of being prized
- 2-of (2 copies): ~19.2% chance at least one copy is prized
- Relevant for ACE SPECs, tech singletons, and combo pieces

The Mega Sharpedo ex / Dusknoir example has 7 singletons, each with a 10% prized risk: Maximum Belt, Dusknoir, Mega Signal, Dawn, Binding Mochi, Rare Candy, and Energy Recycler. Seven independent 10% risks means a meaningful chance that at least one key card is prized in any given game.

## Turn curve

The T1-T4 columns show the cumulative probability of seeing at least one copy by the end of each turn, assuming you go second (draw one card per turn after the opening hand).

This is the key metric for deciding between copy counts. Example: deciding between 2 and 3 copies of Rare Candy.

- 2 copies: open 1+ = 22.1%, by T3 = 29.5%
- 3 copies: open 1+ = 31.5%, by T3 = 41.3%

The third copy adds roughly 10 percentage points to your T3 odds — meaningful when the Stage 2 evolution is your primary attacker.

## Asking the agent

The agent has access to `analyze_deck_probability` as a tool and can reason about consistency questions in the context of your full deck.

```
How consistent is this deck?

Should I run 2 or 3 copies of Rare Candy?

What's the prize risk on my singletons?

If I cut a Buddy-Buddy Poffin to add a third Salvatore, how does that affect consistency?
```

## Combo probability (future)

Joint probability — the chance of seeing two or more specific cards together in the opening hand — is computable from the multivariate hypergeometric distribution but is not yet implemented in the CLI.

<Callout variant="info">
  Multi-card combo analysis is tracked in the project backlog. For now, ask the agent — it can reason through joint probabilities manually when you describe the combo.
</Callout>
